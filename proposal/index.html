Overview

Dear is bootstrapped. No investors, no planned exit. The way you share in what we build is phantom equity: a written contract giving you a cut of Dear's earnings or sale proceeds, paid in cash when triggers fire, vesting over time.

Not real ownership of Elsewhere Index LLC. No shares, no votes, no K-1, no tax obligations until money flows. A written promise that scales with the company. If Dear works, this is real. If Dear doesn't, this costs you nothing.

Cut A · if/when Dear is sold

Your % of net proceeds if/when Dear is sold. Sale here means a sale of Dear specifically (the app, the brand, the IP, the user base, the App Store and Play Store listings, anything Dear-related), not a sale of Elsewhere Index LLC. Net = sale price minus broker fees, legal costs, and any Dear-related debt outstanding. If Dear is never sold, this is zero.

In Q4 2026 or Q1 2027, if Dear has real revenue, we revisit converting this to BSPCE through a French SAS subsidiary. Same percentage, roughly 30% all-in tax instead of ~62%. Roughly doubles the net to you on the same sale event.

Cut B · monthly revenue share

Your floor % of Dear's monthly net revenue above $5,000 MRR, paid monthly. Net = revenue after Apple's platform cut and infrastructure costs. Below $5K MRR, zero. You earn nothing from Cut B until Dear is generating real revenue.

Grows to the higher rate if MRR sustains $25,000+ for 60 consecutive days. That's the milestone ramp.

Vesting

Both cuts vest on the same schedule: 4 years, 12-month cliff.

The first 12 months are the cliff. Nothing vests during the cliff. Leave before month 12 and you forfeit everything. At month 12, 25% vests immediately. From there, 1/48th vests each month through month 48.

Active engagement

Vesting requires active engagement, defined loosely: at least one substantive deliverable per month, or ownership of one shared artifact (a dashboard, a content calendar, a weekly memo, or similar). No hour tracking.

If you go silent for a full quarter, vesting pauses for that quarter and resumes when you re-engage. If Dear shuts down or stops operating, your vested portion stays vested.

What this is not

  • Not real ownership of Elsewhere Index LLC. You hold no membership units.
  • Not voting rights or board representation.
  • Not a K-1. Income from Dear, when it flows, comes through your portage employment, not as partnership distribution.
  • Not an obligation. If Dear never makes meaningful money, you owe nothing back.
  • Not a salary. While you are part-time and unpaid, the phantom equity vests but pays nothing. Cash flows only when triggers fire.
