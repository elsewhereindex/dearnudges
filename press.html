App Overview

Dear: Stay in Touch

One gentle nudge a day to text the people you care about. 15+ voice personas across English, French, and Spanish. Free, private, no accounts.

Founder

Earl Hoover is a former management consultant turned solo developer, from Dallas and currently based in Paris. He built Dear after realizing eight months had passed since he'd seen one of his best friends, and neither of them had noticed.

Philosophy

Dear is built on one principle: no guilt, ever (except from Nonna). There are no streaks, no "missed" nudges, no counters, no backlog. Every day is a clean slate.

If you don't reach out today, the person simply appears again another day. The app never tells you that you failed. It just quietly keeps showing up.

The voice personas exist to make the nudge feel like a friend reminding you, not a calendar alert. Sherlock's deductions, Nonna's gentle guilt, Cyrano's declarations. The goal is a smile, then a text.

Key Facts

  • Price: Free (10 people), $0.99/mo unlimited
  • Languages: English, French, Spanish
  • Voice personas: 15+ (Sherlock Holmes, Nonna, Cyrano, Don Quijote, and more)
  • Privacy: Data Not Collected. No accounts, no tracking, no analytics.
  • Platform: iOS 17+, iPhone and iPad
  • Built with: SwiftUI, SwiftData, WidgetKit. Zero third-party dependencies.
  • App Store: apps.apple.com/app/id6760280035

Research

  • WHO (2023): Loneliness declared a global health threat, linked to an estimated 871,000 deaths per year.
  • Peggy Liu (2022): People consistently underestimate how much others appreciate hearing from them, especially when reaching out "just because."
  • Dunbar's social layers: Humans organize relationships in concentric frequency tiers. Dear mirrors this structure, letting users set per-person contact frequency.
