Privacy Policy

Effective June 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Elsewhere Index LLC (“Elsewhere Index,” “we,” “us”) handles information in connection with the Whim mobile app, published in some regions as ¡Whim!, ¡Antojo! (es-MX), and ¡Lubie! (fr-FR) (the “App”). It is written to be readable. Where we have been informal, the substance still governs.

This policy explains our privacy practices. It works alongside our Terms of Service, which govern your use of the App. Where this policy refers to your purchases, your submissions, moderation, or your responsibilities for real-world acts, the full terms live in the Terms of Service.

1. The short version

  • Whim gives you one small real-world thing to do each day. You scratch a card to reveal it.
  • We do not ask for your name, email, or phone number to use the App. Your identity to us is an anonymous account ID.
  • Your journal and progress live first on your device, and we keep a mirror copy in our cloud so you do not lose it.
  • We collect a small amount of usage and diagnostic data to keep the App working and to make it better.
  • Purchases are handled by RevenueCat together with Apple and Google. We never see your full payment card details.
  • We do not sell your personal data. We do not run third-party advertising inside the App.
  • You can export the data associated with your account, and you can ask us to delete it, from inside the App.

Whim is intended for people 16 and older. Content is light and occasionally flirty, but mild. The App is not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a person under 16 has provided us information, contact us at earl@dearnudges.com and we will take reasonable steps to delete it where it can be identified.

3. What we collect and why

We try to collect as little as possible. Here is what we collect.

a. Your anonymous identity

When you first open or use the App, we create an anonymous account for you through Firebase Authentication. This gives you a random account ID (a “uid”). It is not tied to your name, email, or phone number. We use it only to associate your journal and settings with your device so they survive reinstalls and can be restored.

b. Your activity in the App (“local-first, with a cloud mirror”)

The App is local-first: your journal, your day count (your streak / number of whims completed), your saved preferences, and your theme color are stored on your device first.

We also keep a mirror copy in Cloud Firestore (a Google/Firebase database) so your history is backed up and can be restored on a new device. This mirror is keyed to your anonymous uid and includes: - which whims you were shown and which you marked as done (completion is honor-system; we do not verify, track your location, or check that you actually did the act), - your day count and streak, - your app settings and chosen theme, - any acts you submit (see below).

c. Acts you submit (“pitch to the deck”)

If you pitch an act for the shared deck, we store the text you submit, linked to your anonymous uid so we can vet it, attribute reports to the right item, and remove it if needed. Please do not put personal or identifying information about yourself or anyone else into a submission. See Section 6 for how submissions are reviewed and moderated.

d. Reports

If you report an act as objectionable, we record that a report was made (and which act), tied to your uid for abuse prevention. We do not permit objectionable, unsafe, or abusive content, and we work to remove it. An act that draws repeated reports may be flagged or removed pending review.

e. Usage and diagnostic data

We collect usage and diagnostic data through Firebase, including: app opens, which screens and core features are used, taps on core features, crash and error reports, and device and operating-system information. Some of this data may be linked to your anonymous account ID. We use it to keep the App stable, diagnose problems, and decide what to improve. We do not use it to build advertising profiles, and we do not run third-party advertising inside the App.

f. Purchase information

When you buy a subscription, pack, color, bundle, or re-roll, the transaction is processed by Apple or Google and managed through RevenueCat. We receive confirmation of what you bought and your entitlement status (for example, whether Whim+ is active). We do not receive or store your full credit card number or financial account details.

4. Purchases, subscriptions, and billing

The daily card is free. Some extras are paid, including a Whim+ subscription, one-time act packs, colors, a bundle, and a consumable re-roll. Subscriptions auto-renew until cancelled.

Prices, any free-trial offers, and the terms of each purchase are shown in the App at the point of sale and are controlled by the Apple App Store or Google Play. Payment is charged to your Apple ID or Google account. You manage and cancel subscriptions in your Apple App Store or Google Play account settings, not inside Whim. Restore Purchases is supported in the App. Refunds are handled by Apple or Google under their policies, not by us.

This section summarizes how purchases relate to your data. The complete purchase and subscription terms are in our Terms of Service.
